eSpace Visualisation

This visualisation was written by Joshua Hollick using data from Curtin's eSpace. Thanks to Janice Chan and Daniel Piczak for access to data and the original idea.

For more details please see the paper:
 Joshua Hollick, Pauline Joseph, Peter Green, Janice Chan, "Research network visualisation of co-authorship at Curtin" (in preparation).

This visualisation shows coauthorship within Curtin University. Each node is a person, links are created between people that have coauthored a paper. The strength of these links is proportional to the number of papers coauthored.

Controls are as follows:
  • Use the mouse and WASD to look/move around, Hold shift to speed up movement.
  • Q & E rotate the screen anticlockwise and clockwise respectively.
  • 1-5 enable/disable faculties
  • I, O, P show colouring based on different things
    • I - Faculty Colours
    • O - Degree Centrality, shows nodes and links that have lots of connections
    • P - Betweenness Centrality, essentially shows the most important nodes and links based on the whole graph connectivity.
  • Press 'R' to start/stop the charge spring model (works like play/pause)
  • "Reset State" resets to the default. This doesn't overwrite your previous save so to go back just exit and restart to restore.

Important Note: The data contained in this visualisation is from a limited period and may be incomplete. While the values shown are indicitive they should not be taken as a true representation of research output without further investigation.
